1️⃣ **Microsoft is turning cost cuts into a managed exit**

Microsoft is offering its first voluntary buyout in 51 years to eligible U.S. staff, reportedly covering up to 7% of the local workforce, while simplifying manager reviews and pay options.

💡 **Why it matters**

This is not only headcount control. It is a quieter way to reset talent mix, management layers and performance rules without another layoff spectacle.

☕ **Coffee talk**

If people can self-select out, do you know which expertise your model cannot lose?

---

2️⃣ **Netflix is showing what discipline looks like after a miss**

After dropping its Warner bid, Netflix authorized another $25 billion buyback on April 22, on top of the $6.8 billion still unused from its 2024 plan.

💡 **Why it matters**

The signal is strategic hygiene: walk away from scale you do not need, then redeploy capital with a clearer thesis.

☕ **Coffee talk**

When the big deal dies, does your team know what you stop chasing the next morning?

---

3️⃣ **Software teams now have to prove AI changes the P&L**

IBM and ServiceNow both beat expectations on April 23, but IBM still fell 10.3% and ServiceNow nearly 15% as investors questioned whether software vendors can defend growth once AI starts doing the work.

💡 **Why it matters**

Tolerance for AI theatre is dropping. Leaders now need workflow, margin or headcount evidence, not another copilot slide.

☕ **Coffee talk**

Which AI project in your company still has excitement but no math behind it?
